For TFMs — what needs attention at the program level
Key signals + recommended actions
Improve Class timing is hurting East Coast students — 86 mentions. Students in EST are regularly in class until 11 PM. Consider earlier slots or recorded alternatives for those sections.
Improve Breakout room disengagement is the #1 complaint (105 mentions). Silent rooms, unresponsive peers, and no TF presence are common. Ideas from students: stable groups, TF presence throughout, smaller room sizes (2–3 people), pre-formed groups.
Improve 11.7% of students (122) rated their support feeling ≤ 3/5. Proactively reach out to students who feel unsupported — especially those who haven't attended office hours or study halls.
Improve "Made friends" scored the lowest identity metric at 3.32/5. Students want deeper peer connections beyond random breakout groups. Persistent groups or community channels could help.
Great 80% plan to take another CodePath course. This retention signal is excellent for a Week 3 check-in and confirms the program is landing well overall.
Great Belonging (4.47), Place in Tech (4.46), and Want to Work in Tech (4.73) are all very high — the program is clearly reinforcing mission-aligned identity outcomes.

Summary — TL;DR for leadership
GreatNPS of +47, 80% planning to return, and strong belonging/identity scores signal a healthy program. Staff responsiveness and lecture quality are standout strengths.
